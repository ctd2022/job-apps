This research document analyzes key patterns from industry leaders (LinkedIn, Otta, Wellfound, etc.) to inform the design of your "Local Matcher" application.

## **Executive Summary: The "Candidate-First" Pivot**

The biggest shift in 2024-2025 UX is moving from **"Search & Filter"** (Candidate does the work) to **"Match & Explain"** (AI does the work).

* **Old Way (Indeed/Monster):** Infinite scroll of job cards.
* **New Way (Otta/Cord):** "Daily Picks" or "Top Matches" with explicit reasons *why* they were chosen.

## **2. CV & Profile Builders (Guided Input)**

*How platforms get high-quality data without annoying users.*

### **Pattern A: The "Parser + Verify" (Indeed/LinkedIn)**

1. **Upload:** User drags PDF.
2. **Ghosting:** The UI "ghosts" the parsed text into form fields.
3. **Verification:** The user only edits fields the parser was unsure about (highlighted in yellow).

### **Pattern B: The "Mad Libs" Summary (Polywork/Wellfound)**

Instead of a blank "Summary" box, they use a structured sentence builder:

> "I am a **[Senior Engineer]** interested in **[FinTech]**. I have **[5]** years of experience using **[Python, React]**."

**üèÜ Recommendation for Your App:**
Since you are using Llama locally:

1. **Auto-fill:** Parse the uploaded CV.
2. **The "Gap" Prompt:** If the CV is missing a key section (e.g., "Projects"), explicitly ask: *"I noticed you didn't list any side projects. Want to add one to boost your score?"*

## **3. Match Explanations (The "Black Box" Solution)**

*This is the most critical section for your "Polish."*

### **LinkedIn: The "Venn Diagram" Approach**

* **UI:** A dedicated "How you match" card on the job page.
* **Visual:**
* **‚úÖ Top Skills:** "You have 8/10 skills." (Clickable list).
* **‚ö†Ô∏è Missing:** "Add **Docker** to your profile to appear in more searches."


* **Psychology:** It treats the CV as a living document, encouraging immediate editing.

### **Otta: The "Narrative" Match**

* **UI:** Instead of a score, they show "Why we think it's a match."
* **Copy:** *"This role is in **EdTech** (your interest), requires **Python** (your top skill), and is **Remote** (your preference)."*
* **Badge:** They categorize matches: "Moonshot", "Safe Bet", "Strong Match."

**üèÜ Recommendation for Your App:**
Implement the **"Match Card"**:

* **Header:** Your Match Score (e.g., "Strong Contender").
* **Body:** A generated bullet list (using your Llama integration):
* *"‚úÖ Your experience at [Company A] is highly relevant."*
* *"‚úÖ You possess 4 of the 5 'Must-Have' technical skills."*
* *"‚ö†Ô∏è Missing Evidence: 'Team Leadership' is required but not found in your CV."*

## **4. Dashboards & Tracking**

*How candidates see their status.*

### **The Standard (Greenhouse/Lever)**

* **Status:** Applied -> Phone Screen -> Interview -> Offer.
* **Problem:** It's passive. "Applied (2 weeks ago)" implies rejection but doesn't confirm it.

### **The "Active" Dashboard (Hired/Cord)**

* **Metric:** "Response Rate." They show how responsive the *company* is (e.g., "This company responds to 85% of applicants within 4 days").
* **Action:** "Withdraw" or "Nudge" buttons.

**üèÜ Recommendation for Your App:**
Add a **"Optimize"** column to your dashboard.

* If a match score is < 70%, show a button: **"Fix Resume"**.
* Clicking it highlights exactly which keywords to add to target that specific role.
