# **1) Job Application Flows & Friction Points**

## **Common Patterns**

**Multi-stage flows**

* Filter → Job card list → Job detail → Apply
* Option to apply with saved CV/profile (one click)
* Optional pre-screening questions

**Application entry points**

* From job list
* From job detail page
* Via recommendations (“role for you”)

## **Friction Points**

| Issue                                 | Platforms Seen   | UX Impact                 |
| ------------------------------------- | ---------------- | ------------------------- |
| Auto-redirect to external ATS         | LinkedIn, Indeed | User loses context        |
| Required form fields without guidance | Most boards      | High abandonment          |
| Multiple login/signup gates           | Otta, Talent.io  | Friction before discovery |
| Resume upload resets form             | Glassdoor        | Re-entry frustration      |

## **Patterns to Leverage**

✔ Clear **progress indicators** (e.g., “Step 1 of 3”)
✔ **Prefill data** from profile/CV to reduce typing
✔ **Save progress** automatically mid-apply

## **Recommendations**

* Show **estimated time to complete** application upfront (e.g., “5 min to apply”)
* Lock external redirects behind a modal warning + option to preview form step

# **2) CV/Profile Builders & Guided Input**

## **Patterns**

### **Text Driven Builders**

* LinkedIn: Free-form headline, summary, experience
* Totaljobs/Reed: Form fields for each section

### **Guided Templates**

* Hired/Otta: Structured profile prompts (skills, role preferences)
* Wellfound: Guided startup-specific traits

### **Real-time Feedback**

* LinkedIn highlights profile completeness
* Otta suggests missing preferences

## **Friction Points**

* Lack of **example content** for sections
* No **progressive disclosure** (users overwhelmed by all fields at once)
* Little help on **skill specificity** (e.g., “Java vs JavaScript”)

## **Recommendations**

* Use **smart defaults** and **inline help** with examples
* Break builder into **micro-tasks** (Focus on one section at a time)
* Add **completion meter + quality indicators** (e.g., “Add 3 skills to be 80% complete”)

# **3) Skills Assessment & Verification**

## **Patterns**

### **Self-declared Skills**

* Most job boards allow keyword skills on profile

### **Verified & Assessed Skills**

* Triplebyte: Tech assessments
* Hired: Optional technical screening
* Wellfound: Question sets for product/design roles

## **Friction Points**

* Unclear **what standard** assessment means (no benchmark)
* Frequent **drop-off mid-test**
* Repetitive questions across platforms

## **Recommendations**

* Provide a **clear description** of what the assessment measures
* Use **micro-assessments** (2-3 min tasks) — less intimidating, higher completion
* Display **percentile or comparative score** (e.g., “Scored higher than 72% of applicants”) with context

# **4) Job Matching / Recommendation Algorithms**

## **Patterns**

### **Explicit Matching**

* Otta: “Why this role fits you”
* Wellfound: skills + location + interests

### **Implicit Matching**

* LinkedIn: suggested jobs based on profile activity
* Indeed: “recommended for you”

## **Friction Points**

* **Opaque matches** with no explanation
* Repeated irrelevant suggestions
* Users cannot correct feedback (“Not relevant” doesn’t learn well)

## **Good Explainability Examples**

✔ Otta highlights *top match factors* (role type, stack, culture)
✔ Hired shows *interest level + skill alignment score*

## **Recommendations**

* Show **Match Score** (e.g., 78%) with a breakdown:

  * Skills fit
  * Role preferences
  * Past experience
* Add **controls** for users:

  * “Not interested because ___”
  * “Improve recommendations”

# **5) Application Tracking Dashboards**

## **Patterns**

### **Status Overview**

* Submitted
* Viewed
* Interviewing
* Offer

### **Timeline Visualization**

* Simple list
* Some with stages (LinkedIn)

## **Friction Points**

* Lack of clarity on **what next**
* No guidance on next actions
* Too many statuses without meaning

## **Recommendations**

* Use a **progress bar** or **Kanban style** with clear labels
* Add **next suggested action**:

  * “Follow up in X days”
  * “Complete profile to improve matches”

---

# **6) Match Score Transparency**

## **Patterns**

* Many platforms show a % fit
* Rarely show *why*

## **Problems**

* Scores appear **arbitrary**
* No detail on **missing skills vs preferences**

## **Recommendations**

**Transparent scoring UI**
Display three components side by side:

* Skill match: **60%**
* Experience fit: **70%**
* Culture/role pref match: **85%**

Tooltip or expandable area:

* “You lack X skills that 85% of hires had”

---

# **7) “Why You’re a Good Fit” Features**

## **Patterns**

* Text explanations (Otta/Wellfound)
* Highlight how many requirements you meet
* Occasionally show what you’ve matched on

## **Weaknesses**

* Generic: “You meet most qualifications”
* No personalization or guidance on improvement

## **Examples of Good Micro-copy**

* “You match 8/10 must-have skills”
* “Your React experience aligns with role’s stack”

## **Recommendations**

* Show **specific matched items**:

  * Skills
  * Job requirements
  * Culture fits
* Visualize **gaps** and **suggest actions**:

  * Add portfolio link?
  * Take skill assessment?

# **Overall UX Patterns & Recommendations**

### **Strong Patterns to Borrow**

✅ Prefilled forms from profile
✅ Immediate match reasoning
✅ One-click apply (where possible)
✅ Progress saved across sessions
✅ Next-steps guidance on tracking dashboards

### **Usability Issues Across Platforms**

⚠ Opaque matching logic
⚠ Overwhelming forms without examples
⚠ External redirects breaking flow
⚠ Poor mobile experience in complex ATS forms

### **Design Principles for Your UI**

* **Contextual clarity:** show why steps exist and what’s needed
* **Reduce cognitive load:** micro-steps and inline help
* **Actionable feedback:** explain match scores, next steps
* **User control:** let users refine recommendations
